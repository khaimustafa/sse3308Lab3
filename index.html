Welcome to Santorini, Greece

This Cyclades island in the Aegean Sea looks like a place straight out of a fairy tale, with candy-colored houses carved into cliffs, sapphire waters, and chalk-white buildings topped with cobalt-blue domes. Roam the peaceful black-sand beaches or stroll the streets of one of Santorini’s charming villages (start at Imerovigli). And no matter where your day takes you, wind it down in beautiful Oia: It’s world-famous for its sunsets, which cycle through a kaleidoscope of colors in their spectacular nightly show.

Museum of Prehistoric Thera

The Museum of Prehistoric Thira is located in Fira, on the Greek island of Santorini. It mainly displays amazing finds from the ancient city of Akrotiri, a Minoan Bronze Age settlement that was preserved in volcanic ash (similar to Pompeii).

Ammoudi Bay

​Amoudi Bay is a picturesque coastal spot located at the base of the cliffs beneath the town of Oia on the island of Santorini, Greece. Known for its stunning sunsets and traditional charm, it's a popular destination for visitors seeking authentic Greek experiences.​
